Hubble Space Telescope

Exploring the Universe Since 1990

Functionality

The Hubble Space Telescope is a large telescope in space, launched by NASA in 1990. It orbits Earth and captures images of distant galaxies, stars, and planets, providing invaluable insights into the cosmos. It uses a combination of mirrors and sensors to observe different wavelengths of light, including ultraviolet and infrared, allowing scientists to study the origins and evolution of the universe.
